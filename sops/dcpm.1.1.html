DCPM.1.1 Register a Donor or Patient

Procedure Area: Donor Collection Procedures (DCPM)

Version: 6.0

Purpose

To properly identify and register an allogeneic donor who presents for blood donation, an autologous patient who presents for blood collection, or a patient who presents for therapeutic phlebotomy.

Scope

Donor Services and other staff trained to register donors and patients

Materials

  • Computer workstation
  • "Directed Donor" tag, if needed
  • Donation Identification Number (DIN) set
  • "For Autologous Use Only" tag, if necessary
  • Printer
  • Autologous Patient Record (APR), if appropriate
  • Donor Inquiry Record, if needed (initiated in MO.2.1)
  • Patient Acknowledgment, if needed (initiated in DCPM.7.3)
  • Personal Protective Equipment:
    • Closed-toe shoes
  • Permission for Minor to Donate form, if needed
  • Reportable Event Form, if needed (initiated in EMP.3.1)
  • Request for Directed Donation form, if needed (initiated in DCPM.7.1)
  • Single Donation Record (SDR), if appropriate
  • Therapeutic Patient Record (TPR), if appropriate
  • Therapeutic Phlebotomy Order form, if needed (initiated in DCPM.7.3)

Procedure Notes

  • When accessing a patient's enrollment details, do not modify or change information. Only Quality Assurance is authorized to edit enrollment details. Contact Quality Assurance with questions about the order.

  • If the donor appears to have any difficulty understanding English, request that the donor use a qualified interpreter or bilingual screener. If a qualified interpreter or bilingual screener is not available, defer the donor for one day, and encourage the donor to schedule an appointment when an interpreter or bilingual screener is available. See DRCT.3.2 for information on requesting a third-party interpreter.

  • If an allogeneic donor indicates that they are not able to donate due to a risk found on the Donor Educational Materials, ask the donor to self-defer. There is no need to register the donor.

Procedure Steps

This procedure is divided into the following sub procedures:

  • Determine the reason for donation; see 1.1.1, Determine the Reason for Donation.
  • Initiate apheresis paperwork according to 1.1.2, Register the Donor in IBBIS.
  • Check and prime the kit according to 1.1.3, Handle Donation Paperwork.

1.1.3, Handle Donation Paperwork

  1. Pair the DIN set with the printed donation record (SDR, APR, or TPR). Confirm that the numbers on the DIN set match the number printed on the donation record.
  2. Review the ELIGIBLITY field on the SDR for these items:
    1. HLA: if HLA positive (+), do not draw apheresis platelets or plasma.
    2. ROLLING DEF. ALERT, Eval NDD before rebooking: do this:
      1. (i) Assess the donor's weight and the Plasma Loss (mL) indicated on the SDR to determine if today's donation is affected; specifically consider this:
        If donor weighs Then
        175 lbs. or less Evaluate the Plasma Loss (mL) value:
        If this Then do this*
        less than 11,000 Today's donation can proceed as normal. Continue to step 2.b(ii).
        Between 11,000 and 11,500 Make a note that only a single apheresis platelet should be drawn. Continue to step 2.b(ii).
        More than 11,500 Do not draw apheresis platelets. Continue to step 2.b(ii)

        Note: For donations where platelet additive solution (PAS) will be used, the restrictions related to drawing apheresis platelets can be disregarded. Only certain Amicus-drawing locations perform PAS collections.
        More than 175 lbs. Today's donation can proceed as normal. Continue to step 2.b(ii).

      2. If the donor will donate platelet or plasma apheresis today:
        1. Make a note on the SDR about rescheduling. Advise the donor and the phlebotomist that, the donor's next donation date (NDD) may be affected.
        2. Schedule the donor's next appointment for one day and one year after the date printed on the SDR as the "Earliest donation in this period". For example, if the earliest donation in the period is 23 Jun 2014, schedule the donor's next appointment for 24 Jun 2015 or later.

    3. Notes

    • The FDA limits the total plasma loss a donor can incur over a rolling 12-month period. For donors who weigh up to 175 lbs., this limit is 12,000 m L. For donors who weigh more than 175 lbs., this limit is higher.
    • The FDA only allows 24 platelet apheresis donations in a rolling 12-month period.
    • Refer to the Meeting Plasma Loss and Donation Limit Regulations reference document for more information on limits associated with apheresis platelet donations and plasma loss.

  3. Review the IBBIS Donor Comments field on the SDR to determine if additional actions are necessary. See Table 1.

  4. If performing a blood drive at a military location and the allogeneic donor is a civilian, write "civilian" in the IBBIS Donor Comments field on the front of the SDR.

  5. Prepare the donor's bundle, which may include the following:
    • DIN set assigned in the Register a Donor program
    • Permission for Minor to Donate form, if included
    • "Directed Donor" tag, if donor is donating for a directed patient (see Figure 2)
    • "For Autologous Use Only" tag(s), if donor is an autologous patient (see Figure 3)
    • Any other information or support items, such as a coupon book

  6. Attach the donor bundle items to the printed donation record. Place the bundle in the designated area if the interview is not immediately conducted.
